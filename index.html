CCE Internship Programs

Department: 
Experiential Education
Access: 
Writer

Intern all over the world through CCE Internship programs!

The Center for Career Education (CCE) offers several unique opportunities that allow you to explore your career interests all over the world and develop key professional skills that will make you attractive to prospective employers and help you be successful.

Internship Programs

Spring Semester Programs

The application period is now closed. Please check back for Spring 2017 opportunities in Fall 2016.

Summer Programs

See HERE for the summer 2016 CCE Summer Internship Positions!

Columbia Arts Experience (CAE)
Spring-semester internships with arts organizations in New York

Columbia Experience Overseas (CEO)
Summer internships in Amman, Beijing, Hong Kong, Mumbai, London, Shanghai and Singapore

Kenneth Cole Community Action Program @ Columbia University
Spring-semester internships with civic engagement oriented organizations in New York

Columbia College Alumni-Sponsored Student Internship Program
Alumni-Sponsored internships held in New York and other locations during the summer for Columbia College students across a variety of industries.


Virtual Internship Program (VIP)
Spring-semester project-based, virtual internships


Columbia University Global Opportunities (CU GO)
Professional development and networking for students pursuing international experiences.
Startup Internship Program (SIP)
Spring-semester internships with New York-based startup companies
Science Technology Engineering Program (STEP)
Additional professional development and mentoring opportunities for students interning in the STEM fields.

Columbia Exploration Externship (CEE)
Externship program held in New York during Spring Break week in a variety of different industries

Summer CCE Network (SCCEN)
Student and alumni summer support networks for students living and/or interning in Boston, Los Angeles, San Francisco, and Washington, DC

Update Regarding the Columbia Bartending Agency and School of Mixology:

For the rest of 2015, the Columbia School of Mixology will remain closed as Columbia Entrepreneurship and friends of the CBA continue to re-imagine it as a truly student-run entrepreneurial endeavor. Anyone who is interested helping with the transition should reach out to Nathaniel Kelner (nhk2114@columbia.edu). The Columbia Bartending Agency will remain operational through the end of the year and continue to offer employment opportunities to all students who have been selected and trained as bartenders through previous School of Mixology terms. If you are a prospective or returning customer who is interested in booking bartenders, please visit: http://columbiabartending.com/hire-a-bartender.
